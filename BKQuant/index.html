Data input: Use a clear structure before you run analyses—CSV, Excel (export to CSV when needed), or a tidy Markdown table with one row per genotype and consistent column names.

Accuracy: BKQuant computes statistics in your browser; still double-check critical values such as p-values and mean ± SEm (standard error of the mean) when copying into reports—transcription errors are easy even with AI assistance.

BKQuant is inspired by two traditions: the search for structure in data through mathematics, and the patient work of observing life at the scale where heredity becomes visible.

Srinivasa Ramanujan

Ramanujan (1887–1920) found extraordinary patterns in numbers and sought truth in formulas with a clarity that still guides how we build and check quantitative models.

Rosalind Franklin

Franklin (1920–1958) combined experimental rigor with interpretation of molecular images; her work on DNA reminds us that sound measurement—not speculation—is the foundation of biological understanding.
